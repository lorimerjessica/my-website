Who am I?

Jessica Lorimer is a research assistant in the Neuroscience, Ethics and Society Group (Psychiatry, University of Oxford). She works in two Early Intervention Ethics (EIE) teams: Psychosis and Citizens. Jessica holds an undergraduate degree in Psychology from Occidental College (USA) and a masters in Developmental Psychology from Maastricht University (Netherlands). What do I do?

Jessica works on the following research projects:

    Exploring young people’s moral experiences in everyday life using texting.

    Investigating young people's understanding of Alzheimer’s and attitudes towards predictive genetic testing.

    Exploring young people's attitudes towards using different predictive technologies for measuring risk of different conditions.

    Investigating ‘good practice’ in early intervention in psychosis services, from the perspectives of service users and clinicians.

    Exploring the use and ethics of "big data" practices in charitable giving.
